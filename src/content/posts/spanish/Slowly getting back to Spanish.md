Well, it’s been a hot minute! I planned to write here more often, but life—and other hyperfixations—got in the way, so I had to put language learning on the back burner for a little bit. (What’s new? Sigh.)

I was itching to draft a [Life lately](/tags/Life%20lately/) post for some context, but this blog is about language learning first and foremost, so I think language-related updates should take center stage. The miscellaneous stuff can wait.

I didn’t plan to write a Spanish weekly roundup post _at all_. If I did, I’m sure my “routine” would have been more structured, and I’d have attempted to be more productive. But it is what it is. It’s still interesting to see how a “normal” week goes; that is, when life gets in the way, and I’m not deliberately trying to be proactive with my Spanish studies.

Anyway, here’s a summary of what I did last week (June 8 to 14).

## Reading
I resumed and finished reading _Perdida_ (Gone Girl) by Gillian Flynn. I first read it over a decade ago, so I only remember the major plot points, not the finer details. Before picking it up, I also read a bunch of crime/mystery/thriller novels translated into Spanish, which made me appreciate Flynn’s writing way more.

Compared to the typical airport reads, hers is certainly more literary, particularly in the writing style and characterization. It was strange having to highlight so many unfamiliar words on a single page; I’m usually very comfortable with the vocabulary in such books. Thrillers, after all, are my jam, and they tend to be action-packed rather than character-driven, so the storyline is usually easy to follow. But reading _Gone Girl_ in Spanish humbled me and reminded me how limited my vocabulary still is, as well as the importance of embracing ambiguity.

I also continued with _En otras palabras_ (In Other Words) by Jhumpa Lahiri. I’m trying to read this one more slowly; just a few chapters per day. I prefer to take my time with nonfiction books, and Lahiri’s writing is way too beautiful and reflective to be devoured in a few sittings. It demands to be savored.

## Listening
If there’s one thing you should know about me, listening is always my strongest and most practiced skill. I’m simply _obsessed_ with podcasts.

Last week, though, I didn’t listen as much as I normally do.

I only listened to a couple of podcast episodes, then the rest of my listening hours were on YouTube. I binged a bunch of cycling-related videos (my latest obsession). I caught up with some of my go-to YouTubers ([Jess Zamarripa](https://www.youtube.com/@JessZamarripa) and [Raquel PS](https://www.youtube.com/@Raquel_III_YRI_Concept)) and played their latest videos in the background while doing admin tasks and chores. Each video was nearly an hour long, so it was perfect.

## Speaking
I had a one hour iTalki lesson on Tuesday, so I managed to get some speaking practice in!

iTalki classes are also among my latest attempts to get back into Spanish. I haven’t had a lesson since January, but my credits are expiring soon, so I figured it was a sign to pick it back up.

My speaking skills are also getting rusty, so I desperately *need* some conversation practice.

## Writing
Not much writing was done, unfortunately! I didn’t write a single entry in my Spanish journal. I had some writing practice during my iTalki lesson—my tutor had me listen to a short audio, then write a summary and analysis. The topic was exercise, which was very appropriate now that I’ve been doing so much physical activity.

I also sent some messages to my language partner on Discord. Most of them were in Spanish. That counts, right?

## What’s next?
* As you may have noticed, I’ve mostly been reading books translated into Spanish lately. I’ll probably finish _En otras palabras_ this week. After that, I want to focus on books originally written in Spanish instead. I’m gravitating towards _El príncipe de la niebla_ (a re-read, but it’ll be my first time reading it in Spanish), _Reina roja_, or _El tiempo entre costuras_. We’ll see.
* I have an iTalki lesson on Friday.
* I’m hoping to schedule a call or two with my language partners this week.
* I want to listen to more Spanish stuff again. I discovered a new English podcast that I immediately fell in love with, so I’ve been binge-listening to it a lot, but it’s time to rack up more listening hours in Spanish. I’m aiming to listen to at least 30 minutes per day.
* Writing isn’t a priority this week. If I get some writing done, then great! But it’s just a bonus since I want to focus on input instead.

That’s it for now! See you next week, maybe? 😉
